About ScienceEcosystem

Why We’re Building This

Science today is slowed down by a broken system — paywalled publications, scattered tools, and outdated incentives. Researchers spend too much time managing logistics instead of doing research.

ScienceEcosystem is our response. It’s not just a platform, but a new foundation for how science can work: open, efficient, and collaborative from start to finish.

What We Believe

  • Openness — Research should be free to access, share, and reuse.
  • Integrity — Trust grows when methods, data, and review are transparent.
  • Collaboration — Science improves when researchers work together across fields and borders.
  • Equity — Tools and knowledge should be available to everyone, not just elite institutions.

Our Vision

ScienceEcosystem is built for the long term. Over the next years, we aim to transform how science is created, shared, and used. We will replace journals and paywalls with a unified, open infrastructure that serves the entire research community and the public.

  • No more journals: Research is shared as modular, living knowledge—open for review, reuse, and refinement from day one.
  • All scientific knowledge is open: Data, code, methods, commentary, funding, and review are fully accessible and connected.
  • AI works in the background: Smart tools summarize, link, and verify work quietly, without interrupting researchers or taking credit.
  • Collaboration over competition: Scientists, institutions, and private partners contribute to a shared public commons.
  • The paper is replaced: Research is validated through transparency and reproducibility, not journal brands or gatekeeping.
  • Science becomes public infrastructure: As essential and trusted as Wikipedia, nonprofit, and built to last.

We believe scientific knowledge is too important to be hidden, sold, or lost. ScienceEcosystem will always be free, nonprofit, and owned by the communities it serves.

Who It’s For

Researchers, students, educators, funders — anyone who believes science should work better than it does today.

Whether you’re publishing a dataset, reviewing a preprint, or coordinating an interdisciplinary project, our aim is to support the full lifecycle of scientific work, not just the final publication.

Where We're Going

We’re starting small, testing ideas with early adopters, and staying grounded in real research needs. As we grow, our goal is to become a shared infrastructure — not a product to sell, but a system to build with the scientific community.

How We’re Building ScienceEcosystem

We’re designing for steady, thoughtful development, working closely with early users and institutions to guide each step. Research should live as dynamic, modular objects that evolve over time.

2025: Foundation

  • Launch the public website and early messaging
  • Build the MVP: basic document upload, AI‑powered summaries, semantic search
  • Start community outreach across disciplines
  • Begin integration work with ORCID, OpenAlex, and other open infrastructure

2026: Review and Collaboration

  • Introduce open peer review, annotation, and reputation tools
  • Run beta testing with researchers and research groups
  • Pilot funding models (microgrants, community prioritization)
  • Build tools to support reproducibility and shared workspaces

2027: Scale and Impact

  • Full platform release with collaboration, publication, and funding systems
  • Partnerships with institutions, funders, and open science communities
  • Citizen science and public education components
  • Policy translation and global access initiatives
